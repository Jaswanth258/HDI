📐 Algorithmic Formulation

The model utilizes the learned weights and intercept to map development indicators into a continuous decimal output representing the country's development level.

HDI = 0.1453 + 0.0128*(EYS) + 0.0107*(MYS) + 0.0057*(Life_Exp) + 1.9e-6*(GNI) + 2.4e-5*(Country_Enc)
EYS: Expected Years Schooling
MYS: Mean Years Schooling
Life_Exp: Life Expectancy
GNI: GNI per Capita (PPP $)

📈 Exploratory Data Analysis & Visualizations

🔍 Feature Correlation Heatmap

This correlation matrix maps the statistical relationship between all numeric indicators in the training dataset and the target HDI Index. The scores represent Pearson correlation coefficients ranging from -1.0 (perfect negative) to +1.0 (perfect positive).

Key Observations for Video Demo:

  • Education Dominance: Expected Years of Schooling (0.94) and Mean Years of Schooling (0.90) show the highest positive correlations, explaining why the model assigns them the highest coefficients.
  • Strong Health Indicator: Life Expectancy displays a strong positive correlation of 0.81.
  • Income Diminishing Returns: GNI per capita has a lower direct linear correlation (0.59) due to the exponential nature of GDP distribution, which validates the UNDP's methodology of applying logarithmic scaling to economic variables.

📚 Mean Schooling vs HDI Distribution

This strip plot visualizes the distribution of the target HDI Index relative to the Mean Years of Schooling indicator across the training dataset profiles.

Key Observations for Video Demo:

  • Clear Upward Shift: As adult schooling duration increases, the predicted/actual index score shifts steadily upward.
  • High Education Groupings: Countries with mean schooling years above 10.0 years consistently score above the 0.800 threshold, mapping directly into the Very High human development tier.
  • Developing Gaps: Lower schooling brackets (under 6 years) are concentrated below the 0.600 index range, indicating a clear developmental bottleneck.

❤️ Life Expectancy vs HDI Distribution

This strip plot visualizes the distribution of index rankings relative to national life expectancy indicators at birth.

Key Observations for Video Demo:

  • Health Foundation: Represents the demographic core of development. Higher life expectancy reflects robust national healthcare, sanitization, and nutritional standards.
  • Developed Clustering: Developed profiles cluster closely together above the 75 years life expectancy range.
  • Urgent Intervention Zones: Developing countries under 60 years life expectancy are clustered at the bottom left, highlighting target areas that require direct medical interventions.
